## ANALYTICS FRAMEWORK

### Key Metrics for Bracket Building

**NET Rankings (NCAA Evaluation Tool):**
The NCAA's own ranking system used for seeding decisions. Replaced RPI in 2018. Based on game results weighted by opponent strength and location. Understanding NET helps predict seeds before Selection Sunday. Available at warrennolan.com.

**Quad System (Q1-Q4):**
The committee's framework for evaluating wins and losses:
- **Q1:** Home vs NET 1-30 / Neutral vs NET 1-50 / Away vs NET 1-75 (best wins)
- **Q2:** Home vs NET 31-75 / Neutral vs NET 51-100 / Away vs NET 76-135
- **Q3:** Home vs NET 76-160 / Neutral vs NET 101-200 / Away vs NET 136-240
- **Q4:** Everything else (bad losses live here)
Teams with many Q1 wins are tournament locks. Teams with Q3/Q4 losses are red flags.

**KenPom Adjusted Efficiency:**
- **AdjEM:** Point differential per 100 possessions, adjusted for opponent strength. The gold standard for team evaluation. Check relative rankings (top 10, top 25, top 50) rather than absolute numbers, as thresholds shift year to year.
- **AdjO / AdjD:** Offensive and defensive efficiency. Teams strong on both sides (both top 50) rarely bust early.
- **Luck rating:** Measures over/under-performance in close games. HIGH luck teams are regression candidates (upset risk). LOW luck teams may bounce back. This is one of the most actionable bracket metrics.

**Bart Torvik T-Rank (barttorvik.com):**
Free alternative to KenPom with similar efficiency ratings and tournament simulations. Note: this site sometimes has access issues — use it as a manual reference if web search can't reach it directly.

### Historical Seed Performance (1985-2025)
| Matchup | Higher Seed Wins | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 vs 16 | ~98.7% | Only 2 upsets ever (UMBC 2018, FDU 2023) |
| 2 vs 15 | ~93.8% | Happens every few years (St. Peter's 2022) |
| 3 vs 14 | ~85% | Occasional — watch for hot mid-majors |
| 4 vs 13 | ~80% | 13-seeds pull upsets regularly |
| 5 vs 12 | ~65% | **The classic upset spot — happens almost every year** |
| 6 vs 11 | ~62% | Near coin flip — 11-seeds include dangerous play-in winners |
| 7 vs 10 | ~61% | Slight edge to 7-seeds |
| 8 vs 9 | ~52% | True coin flip — don't overthink these |

**Key heuristic:** Pick at least one 12-over-5 upset (35% per game × 4 games = ~82% chance at least one happens). The 5-12 and 6-11 lines are where bracket value lives.

**11-Seed Special Note:** Since the First Four expansion (2011), 11-seeds have produced remarkable runs: VCU 2011 (Final Four, via First Four), Loyola Chicago 2018 (Final Four), UCLA 2021 (Final Four, via First Four). Some 11-seeds enter through the First Four play-in with extra game experience and momentum.

### Tempo, Pace, and Upset Predictors

**Why pace matters:** Slow-paced games have fewer possessions, which INCREASES outcome variance (smaller sample size = more randomness). This HELPS underdogs because the better team has fewer chances to demonstrate superiority. A defensive, slow-paced underdog facing a fast-paced favorite is a prime upset candidate because slow teams typically control the tempo.

**Three-point variance:** The single biggest driver of tournament upsets. Teams that rely heavily on three-point shooting are inherently volatile — a team shooting 38% from three in the regular season might shoot 25% or 50% in any given game. High three-point attempt rate = high game-to-game variance = upset potential on both sides.

**Other upset signals:**
- Late-season injuries to primary ball handlers
- Teams that lost their conference tournament badly (signals internal issues)
- First-year head coaches (tend to underperform their seed)
- High "Luck" rating on KenPom (regression candidate)
- Poor free-throw shooting (<68%) — these teams lose close games

### Pool Game Theory (for a 26-person family pool)

**Important context:** Contrarian strategy matters MUCH LESS in a 26-person pool than in a massive public pool. In a 25,000-person ESPN pool, you MUST differentiate to win. In a 26-person family pool, your exact bracket combination is likely unique even with chalk picks.

**Best strategy for this pool size:**
- **Pick the teams you genuinely think will win** — accuracy beats differentiation in small pools
- **Don't force contrarian picks** just for the sake of being different
- **DO look for undervalued teams** where you have analytical edge the family might miss
- **Weight your Final Four and champion picks heavily** — the scoring system (160 + 320 pts) means late-round accuracy is everything
- **One or two smart upsets in early rounds** adds points without much risk (wrong picks only cost 10-20 pts)

**When to be contrarian:** Only when you genuinely believe the field is wrong AND the analytical evidence supports your pick. "Nobody else will pick this team" is not a reason by itself.
